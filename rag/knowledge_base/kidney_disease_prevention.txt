KIDNEY DISEASE PREVENTION GUIDELINES

Primary Prevention Strategies:
1. Blood Pressure Control
   - Target: <130/80 mmHg for kidney protection
   - ACE inhibitors/ARBs preferred for renal protection
   - Home BP monitoring recommended
   - Sodium restriction: <2,300mg daily, ideally <1,500mg
   - Each 10mmHg systolic reduction slows CKD progression by 30%

2. Diabetes Management
   - A1C target: <7% for kidney protection
   - SGLT2 inhibitors: 30-40% reduction in kidney disease progression
   - GLP-1 agonists: Additional renal benefits
   - Regular glucose monitoring: 3-4 times daily if on insulin
   - Tight control reduces albuminuria by 30-40%

3. Lifestyle Modifications
   - Weight management: BMI 18.5-24.9 kg/m²
   - Regular exercise: 150 minutes/week moderate activity
   - Smoking cessation: Smoking doubles kidney disease progression risk
   - Limit alcohol: <1 drink/day for women, <2 for men
   - Hydration: 2-3 liters water daily (unless contraindicated)

4. Dietary Approaches
   - Protein intake: 0.8-1.0g/kg body weight for CKD prevention
   - Reduce processed foods: High in phosphorus and sodium
   - Potassium monitoring: Especially in advanced CKD
   - Plant-based diets: May slow CKD progression
   - Limit NSAIDs: Can cause acute kidney injury

5. Medication Management
   - Avoid nephrotoxic medications when possible
   - Dose adjust for kidney function
   - Regular medication review with healthcare provider
   - Vaccinations: Annual flu, pneumococcal, hepatitis B
   - Herbal supplements: Many can harm kidneys

Risk Factor Identification:
- Age >60 years
- Diabetes mellitus
- Hypertension
- Family history of kidney disease
- Obesity (BMI >30)
- Smoking history
- Cardiovascular disease
- Autoimmune diseases
- Recurrent kidney infections
- Kidney stones

Screening Recommendations:
- GFR estimation: Annually if high risk
- Urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio: Annually for diabetics
- Blood pressure: Every healthcare visit
- Blood glucose: Every 3 months if diabetic
- Lipid panel: Annually

Early Detection Markers:
- Microalbuminuria: 30-300 mg/g creatinine
- eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m²
- Rising serum creatinine
- Electrolyte abnormalities
- Anemia of chronic disease

Medication Considerations:
- ACE inhibitors/ARBs: First-line for proteinuria
- SGLT2 inhibitors: Renal protection in diabetics
- Diuretics: For fluid management
- Phosphate binders: If hyperphosphatemia
- Erythropoietin: For anemia management

Dietary Restrictions in CKD:
- Sodium: <2,000mg daily
- Protein: 0.6-0.8g/kg in advanced CKD
- Potassium: 2,000-3,000mg if elevated levels
- Phosphorus: 800-1,000mg daily
- Fluid: 2L daily if fluid retention

Complications Prevention:
- Cardiovascular disease: Leading cause of death in CKD
- Bone disease: Monitor calcium, phosphorus, vitamin D
- Anemia: Check hemoglobin every 3 months
- Metabolic acidosis: Monitor bicarbonate levels
- Fluid overload: Daily weight monitoring

Dialysis Preparation:
- Vascular access planning when eGFR <20
- Education about treatment options
- Psychological preparation
- Nutritional counseling
- Family involvement in decision-making

Evidence Base:
- KDIGO guidelines: International kidney disease standards
- SPRINT trial: Intensive BP control renal benefits
- CREDENCE trial: SGLT2 inhibitors in diabetic kidney disease
- DAPA-CKD trial: SGLT2 benefits in non-diabetic CKD

Warning Signs:
- Swelling in legs, ankles, feet
- Changes in urination frequency
- Foamy or bloody urine
- Fatigue and weakness
- Shortness of breath
- Confusion or difficulty concentrating
- Nausea and vomiting
- Muscle cramps

Emergency Situations:
- Acute kidney injury: Sudden rise in creatinine
- Hyperkalemia: EKG changes, muscle weakness
- Volume overload: Severe swelling, shortness of breath
- Uremic encephalopathy: Confusion, seizures

Follow-up Schedule:
- Stage 1-2 CKD: Every 6-12 months
- Stage 3 CKD: Every 3-6 months
- Stage 4 CKD: Every 1-3 months
- Stage 5 CKD: Every 1 month

Quality of Life:
- Exercise programs tailored to kidney function
- Mental health support for chronic illness
- Social work assistance for financial concerns
- Palliative care integration when appropriate
- Advanced care planning discussions
