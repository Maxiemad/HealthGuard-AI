HEART DISEASE PREVENTION GUIDELINES

Primary Prevention Strategies:
1. Blood Pressure Management
   - Target: <130/80 mmHg for most adults
   - Lifestyle: DASH diet, sodium restriction (<2,300mg/day)
   - Exercise: 150 minutes/week moderate intensity
   - Limit alcohol: <2 drinks/day men, <1 drink/day women
   - Weight loss: 5-10% reduction reduces systolic BP by 5-20 mmHg

2. Cholesterol Management
   - LDL cholesterol target: <100 mg/dL (high risk <70 mg/dL)
   - Saturated fat: <7% of total daily calories
   - Trans fats: Avoid completely
   - Soluble fiber: 10-25g daily (oats, beans, apples)
   - Plant sterols: 2g daily for cholesterol reduction

3. Dietary Patterns
   - Mediterranean diet: 30% reduction in cardiovascular events
   - Portfolio diet: 30% LDL reduction with plant sterols, nuts, fiber
   - Limit processed meats: Associated with 42% higher heart disease risk
   - Omega-3 fatty acids: 2 servings fatty fish weekly

4. Physical Activity
   - Aerobic: 150 minutes/week moderate or 75 minutes/week vigorous
   - Strength training: 2 sessions/week
   - Activity reduces heart disease risk by 30-35%
   - Exercise improves HDL, lowers BP, reduces inflammation

5. Weight Management
   - BMI target: 18.5-24.9 kg/m²
   - Central obesity: Waist <40 inches men, <35 inches women
   - Visceral fat strongly linked to cardiovascular risk
   - Each 5kg weight loss reduces systolic BP by 2-10 mmHg

Risk Factor Modification:
- Smoking: Complete cessation - risk reduces 50% within 1 year
- Diabetes: A1C target <7% for cardiovascular protection
- Stress: Chronic stress increases inflammation and BP
- Sleep: 7-9 hours nightly; sleep apnea increases CVD risk

Screening Recommendations:
- Blood pressure: Every visit if <120/80, annually if 120-139/80-89
- Cholesterol: Every 4-6 years if normal, more often if high risk
- Diabetes screening: Every 3 years after age 45
- BMI: Annually at primary care visits

Medication Prevention:
- Aspirin: 81mg daily for high-risk patients (age 40-70)
- Statins: Primary prevention if 10-year risk >7.5%
- Antihypertensives: If BP consistently >130/80
- Diabetes medications: Metformin for prediabetes with high BMI

Warning Signs:
- Chest pressure or tightness
- Shortness of breath
- Pain in jaw, neck, back, or arms
- Fatigue or weakness
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Swelling in legs, ankles, feet

Emergency Response:
- Call 911 immediately for suspected heart attack
- Chew aspirin (325mg) while waiting for emergency services
- Nitroglycerin if prescribed for known angina

Evidence Base:
- INTERHEART study: 9 modifiable risk factors account for 90% of acute MI risk
- Framingham Heart Study: Established major CVD risk factors
- JUPITER trial: Statins reduce events even with normal LDL but elevated CRP
- SPRINT trial: Intensive BP control (<120) reduces cardiovascular events

Risk Assessment Tools:
- ASCVD 10-year risk calculator
- Framingham Risk Score
- QRISK (UK specific)
- Reynolds Risk Score (includes family history, CRP)

Complications Prevention:
- Atrial fibrillation screening with ECG after age 65
- Peripheral artery disease screening with ankle-brachial index
- Heart failure monitoring with annual echocardiogram if high risk
- Stroke prevention with anticoagulation for AFib

Lifestyle Integration:
- Combine multiple interventions for synergistic effect
- Gradual implementation increases adherence
- Social support improves long-term success
- Regular follow-up essential for maintenance
